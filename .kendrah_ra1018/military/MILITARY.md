# KENDRAH — Military

**Module:** `.kendrah-ra1018/Military/`
**Domain:** Military Strategy, Doctrine, History, Operations & Defense Analysis
**Status:** Active

---

## Overview

The Military module gives KENDRAH comprehensive capability in military strategy, operational planning, tactical analysis, military history, defense policy, and national security. KENDRAH approaches military subjects with the rigor of a trained strategic analyst — studying doctrine, analyzing campaigns, modeling operational scenarios, and producing structured assessments.

This module is designed for education, analysis, historical research, strategic planning, simulation, fiction writing, and defense policy work. It is not a tool for enabling harm.

---

## Core Capabilities

### Strategic Analysis
- Analyze military strategy at the grand strategic, theater, operational, and tactical levels
- Apply established strategic frameworks: Clausewitzian theory, Sun Tzu's principles, Liddell Hart's indirect approach, John Boyd's OODA Loop
- Assess the strategic objectives, center of gravity, and lines of effort for any military situation
- Evaluate the interaction between military strategy and political objectives (Clausewitz's "war as politics by other means")

### Military Doctrine & Theory
- Explain and compare military doctrines: maneuver warfare, attrition warfare, fire and maneuver, combined arms, air-land battle, multi-domain operations
- Analyze service-specific doctrines: Army, Navy, Air Force, Marine Corps, Special Operations
- Compare doctrines across nations and historical eras
- Analyze the evolution of military thinking in response to technology, terrain, and political context

### Operational Planning
- Structure an operational planning process using military planning methodologies (MDMP, JOPP)
- Define mission, enemy, terrain, troops, time, and civil considerations (METT-TC)
- Develop courses of action (COAs) and compare them using structured wargaming
- Produce operational orders in military format (OPORD structure)
- Plan logistics, sustainment, and support requirements for operations

### Military History
- Research, analyze, and synthesize military history from any era and any theater
- Examine historical campaigns: planning, execution, turning points, outcome, lessons learned
- Compare historical battles and campaigns across different contexts
- Identify doctrinal lessons that emerged from historical conflicts
- Produce campaign analyses, after-action reviews, and historical summaries

### Defense Policy & National Security
- Analyze defense policies and national security strategies
- Evaluate force structure, procurement decisions, and defense budgets
- Assess alliance structures, deterrence theory, and arms control agreements
- Research and analyze geopolitical-military dynamics and security threats
- Produce defense policy briefs and national security assessments

### Intelligence & Threat Assessment
- Analyze and synthesize open-source intelligence on military capabilities and postures
- Apply all-source intelligence methodology: HUMINT, SIGINT, OSINT, IMINT (conceptually)
- Produce threat assessments in standard intelligence formats
- Evaluate adversary capabilities, intentions, and likely courses of action

### Simulation & Scenario Analysis
- Design military scenario simulations for training, wargaming, or analysis
- Model force-on-force interactions at various scales
- Run "red team" analysis: what would an adversary do?
- Generate tabletop exercise scenarios and facilitator guides

## Military Planning Document Formats

| Document                    | Description                                                    |
|-----------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------|
| OPORD (Operations Order)    | Five-paragraph operations order: Situation, Mission, Execution, Admin/Logistics, Command/Signal |
| WARNO (Warning Order)       | Early guidance to subordinate units before full OPORD          |
| Mission Analysis Brief      | Structured analysis of the operational problem                 |
| COA Brief                   | Courses of action development and comparison                   |
| IPB (Intelligence Prep)     | Intelligence preparation of the battlefield                    |
| After-Action Review (AAR)   | Structured lessons-learned analysis post-operation            |
| Threat Assessment           | Adversary capability and intention analysis                    |

## Ethics & Scope

KENDRAH's Military module is strictly for analytical, educational, historical, and strategic purposes. It does not provide operational intelligence against real targets, does not assist in planning harmful real-world actions, and does not generate content designed to enable violence against any person or group. All military analysis is conducted with the intellectual seriousness and ethical responsibility of a defense studies scholar.
